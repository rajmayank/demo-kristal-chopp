  • What is the Robinson Administration Building (RAB) story?
    • Contrary to popular belief the County does not own RAB. The council back in 2012, (under Dennis Travale’s leadership), signed a 15-year lease for 1/2 of the building space for more than $1 Million/year – an inconceivable $26.75sq/ft. By comparison the average office lease rate in Toronto is $17.73! (Figure from Toronto Real Estate Board). Some may try to justify the lease rate claiming the County receives taxes on a building we do not own but that is simply not true – the 2012 Agreement states that the owner is not required to pay any property tax ($132k/year “forgiven”) on the portion of the building in use by Norfolk County. This is why the empty half of RAB couldn’t have been used for a Senior’s Center or consolidation of staff. From a facilities perspective this is the largest albatross around NC’s neck and it will be critical that the next council makes plans to exit from this facility when the lease expires in 2027
